The Precedent

Bowie Bonds

How David Bowie invented celebrity-backed securities â€” and why it matters for indie music today.

In January 1997, David Bowie did something no musician had ever done. He walked into a room with investment banker David Pullman and emerged with $55 million â€” not from a record deal, not from a tour, but from the future.
Bowie securitised the future royalties of his first 25 albums â€” everything from "Space Oddity" to "Let's Dance." The bonds paid 7.9% annually over 10 years, backed entirely by the income those recordings would generate. Prudential Insurance bought the entire issue. It was, at the time, unprecedented.

What Bowie Bonds proved

Creative output has financial value
A song generates measurable, recurring revenue. That revenue stream can be the basis for financial instruments.
You don't have to sell to monetise
Bowie didn't sell his catalogue. He borrowed against it. Kept ownership, got liquidity.
Revenue-backed = risk-bounded
Backed by actual revenue, not speculation. When the industry changed, the bonds still paid out.
Scale matters less than structure
The key ingredients: predictable revenue, transparent reporting, aligned incentives. Works at any scale.

The Economics

How YouTube ad revenue works, what uplift means, and realistic return scenarios.

How YouTube AdSense works

When a monetised video plays an ad, the creator earns revenue. YouTube takes ~45%, creator keeps ~55%. Revenue measured as RPM â€” earnings per 1,000 views.

Music content typically generates RPMs between $0.25â€“$4.00. An artist with 10,000 daily views at $2.00 RPM earns ~$20/day or $600/month.

ZiggyDust tracks this via YouTube's Analytics API, authenticated via OAuth. Read-only access. We observe, we don't touch.

The baseline

Before a drop goes live, we compute the baseline: average daily channel-wide YouTube AdSense revenue over the 30 days before activation.

Only channel-wide revenue above baseline counts as uplift â€” capturing cross-views and discovery of older content. Earning $5/day before, $12/day during = $7/day uplift. Backers share the $7, not the $12.
